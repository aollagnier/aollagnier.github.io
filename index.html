about me

Since February 2023, I am a Junior Fellow in AI at the 3IA institut and I am a member of the joint Inria-CNRS-UCA Wimmics team. From january 2021 to january 2023, I was already a part of the Wimmics team as a post-doctorant fellow in computer science. Over the last past years, I have particularly focused my researches on identifying and preventing cyberbullying on social media and also in developping innovative methods to address high-level linguistic analyses in ML.
In November 2017 I received the Ph.D. in computer science from the Aix-Marseille UniversitÃ© (France), with a thesis focused on developing a content-based recommender system using natural language processing techniques. I conducted my PhD research in the Data, Information content MAnagement Group (DIMAG) in the LIS (Computer and Systems Laboratory) at Marseille (France). As a part of my work, I also had the opportunity to collaborate with one of the world's leading platforms in electronic publishing in the field of Humanities and Social Sciences (HSS): OpenEdition. My contribution to BILBO (Automatic reference labelling) resulted in a publicly available tool which is still one of the main components of digital tools developed in the OpenEdition Lab.
In 2018, I moved to the University of Exeter (UK) for a two-year postdoc in the Social & Environmental Data Analysis (SEDA) lab to work on a career coach for personalised learning. This work resulted in a fully integrated self-directed career exploration tool called C3-IoC whose a publicly version is available online.
